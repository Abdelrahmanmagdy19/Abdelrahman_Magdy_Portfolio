About Me

My story started during my final year of high school. Like most science students, I had one big goal: getting into medical school. I worked hard, gave it everything I had, and truly believed I could make it. But when the results came out, I didn’t.

That day felt like the end of the world.

I wasn’t just disappointed. I felt like I had let everyone down. My family, who believed in me. Myself. I couldn’t give them the big moment we had all been dreaming of. For weeks, I stayed quiet, lost in sadness, with no idea what came next. Then when university placements opened, things got even harder. My dream wasn’t even on the table anymore.

But then something unexpected happened.

That year, the government made a decision to allow science students to apply for Computer Science programs. Honestly, I didn’t care at the time. I had zero interest in programming or computers. I only knew it had something to do with code. My brother stepped in. He encouraged me to apply and even submitted the application for me.

So I joined the Faculty of Computer Science not knowing a thing about it and not expecting much either.

My first year was rough. I didn’t understand the system, the lectures, or what I was even supposed to be learning. I’d hear classmates talk about programming languages and the apps they’d already built, and I felt completely lost. Like I didn’t belong. But I stayed. I had no other path.

That year was all about survival.

In my second year, I made a choice to start exploring. I listened, I watched, I read. I tried to understand how things worked, and eventually I picked web development. Not because I loved it, but because it seemed like the most common thing people were doing. I just wanted to find a way in.

By third year, something shifted. I finally started to get it. How the system worked, what I was studying, and more importantly, the potential of it all. I realized that Computer Science is the future, and the tech field is full of opportunity. That year, we had to build a mobile app for a project, and that’s when I discovered Flutter.

I fell in love with it instantly. The performance, the cross-platform capabilities, the elegance.

From that moment, I wasn’t just following the crowd anymore. I had found my direction.

I dove deep into mobile development and became truly passionate about building beautiful, high-performance apps. Today, I’m a Flutter developer with 1.5 years of hands-on experience, turning ideas into smooth, cross-platform mobile apps that work on both Android and iOS. I specialize in building responsive and adaptive UIs, integrating Firebase, working with RESTful APIs, and managing state with Cubit and Bloc, all built on solid OOP principles and MVVM architecture.

In my final year, everything came full circle. I had chosen this path for myself, found something I love, and learned how to take charge of my own future. I built my graduation project with my best friend, just the two of us, and even though it was tough, we pulled through. When we got an A with distinction, I finally felt like everything I’d gone through was worth it.

And I took one more step.

I launched my own YouTube channel to teach others what I had learned. I create content about Flutter and programming fundamentals, helping beginners take their first confident steps in tech because I know exactly how it feels to be lost, and I want to help others find their path faster than I did.

When I graduated, I wasn’t just proud of my degree. I was proud of who I had become.

What once felt like failure turned out to be the start of something much bigger.
